The Story of Frankenstein’s HTML and CSS monster.

Once upon a time, in a little Bavarian town, somewhere at the bottom of a mountain, lived a man known by the name of Dr. Frankenstein.

Dr. Frankenstein was a tech savvy web dev, living his days just tinkering away on an old banged up MacBook pro. The Dr lived alone, and although he had many friends on reddit he was starting to long for someone a bit closer to home. One day, he got a crazy idea... What if I could create a human within my web browser, to live with me and keep me from getting lonely...? So he opened up VS code and started hacking away at the keyboard “Typing noises”. First thing, he types up a head, then slaps on a body, chucks in a few asides and finally adds a footer. Dr Frankenstein, shaking from excitement opens up the browser to see his new friend, but what does he see? Nothing but the bare bones of his new friend. Devastated and wondering where he gone wrong he took off to his study to bury himself in stackoverflow, determined to find a way to bring his new friend to life. Weeks past, when he stumbles across a little link, barely still clickable. It takes him to W3schools.com where he finds a little tool they are referring to as CSS. Excited again, Dr Frankenstein heads to his computer and starts typing away “Excessively loud typing noises”.

“I’ve done it!” He says to himself! He does his final commit and pushes his code to github. Once again, riddled with excitement he opens the browser, heads to his github and opens the github pages file called newFriend.html and there she is! Dr. Frankenstein’s new friend. She has long blonde hair, a green dress on, and little red ribbon on her head. The Dr is Exceptionally excited, he tells all his friends about her, and can’t wait to introduce her. But soon he notices, somethings wrong. When he says hello, or clicks on her she doesn’t move or do anything. It seems she’s not alive. So Dr. Frankenstein heads back to stackoverflow to see what he can do to give life to his new found friend... That part of the story will have to wait until the next chapter.

An explanation of the difference between accessing data from arrays and objects. Arrays are ordered objects and can be accessed using a numbered reference, starting from zero. For example array[0] will get the first item in the array, while array[9] would return the tenth. Objects however have Key - Value pairs and you can look up an individual object by searching for its key. Everyday life is full of objects which essentially act in the same way. For example a human, it has a hair property, height, eye colour etc. and object for a human might look like the following:

Const Human = {
Name : “Seb”,
Height : 158,
EyeColor : “Blue”
Interests : [ “Coding”, “Economics”, “Mountain Biking”, “Climbing”, “Hunting” ]
}

As you can see an object can hold properties of all data types (including other objects). To access Seb’s eye color, you can use ether dot or bracket notations. Dot notation would look like the following: Human.EyeColor while bracket notation would look like human[‘EyeColor’]

Now onto functions. Functions are a way of organising code and making it repeatable. They generally take an input and return an output. It is considered good practice for the input to not be altered but the function, but rather return a new object.
